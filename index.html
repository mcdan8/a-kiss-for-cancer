Our Mission

Reaching the people least likely to be reached.

A K.I.S.S. for Cancer was created by our founder, Britt'nee Willis, to empower the less fortunate and disadvantaged to become active in knowing their health status — through education, community gatherings, and local charity events that meet people where they are.

Britt'nee was 29 when she was diagnosed with stage 4 colon cancer, after being told she was too young for it. This foundation is her answer: no one else should find out too late.

Get Screened

Colon cancer doesn't check your age.

Colorectal cancer is rising in younger adults, and the early signs are easy to dismiss. Don't ignore:

  • A lasting change in bowel habits
  • Blood in your stool
  • Ongoing abdominal pain, cramping, or bloating
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue that doesn't go away

Screening is recommended starting at age 45 — earlier if colon cancer runs in your family. If a parent or sibling has had it, talk to your doctor now about when to start. One appointment can be the whole difference.

Schedule the check-up. Then ask someone you love to do the same.

K.I.S.S. Stories

Our founder, our first story: Britt'nee Willis.

Britt'nee Willis, of Griffin, Georgia, visited her local hospital and was immediately rushed to surgery after an alarming surprise. She was diagnosed with colon cancer at the age of 29.

Because of her age, the hospital staff initially treated her only for ulcers. The signs were there — but no one tested her, simply because they felt she was too young. By the time a physician on call recognized her symptoms and ran the test, her condition had worsened to stage 4.

Her father had faced the same disease before her. Britt'nee understood what that meant for her seven children, her five siblings, and a family full of cousins. So in the middle of her own fight, she founded A K.I.S.S. for Cancer.

Britt'nee passed away in 2015, before she could see her foundation grow. Her words below are hers forever — and her family carries the rest.

“I only went to the doctor when there was something wrong. I never took it upon myself to schedule regular appointments… until I was told that I had cancer. I now know that it's not too late — however, a lot could've been prevented had I gone regularly for check-ups and yearly exams.”

— Britt'nee Willis

“My goal is to take the resources that I've acquired through experience and translate it into something that anyone can understand. I want to reach out to those that are unwilling or unable, and teach them that knowing your health status makes a big difference in survival. Because I truly believe that Knowledge IS Saving Someone.”

— Britt'nee Willis
